Wildfire Hazard Potential Visualization

Hover over a specific county to see some additional demographic data points about it and toggle between the two maps to see the colors change for each county.

Average Wildfire Hazard Potential(AWHP):
The AWHP is the average of all the WHP scores registered for a specific county.

Weighted Average Wildfire Hazard Potential (PW-AWHP):
The PW-AWHP takes the AWHP and adjusts the value based on population density in a county. A county with higher population density will be weighted more heavily than a county with lower population density.

By weighing the AWHP of a county against its population average it is possible to get an estimate of the impact upon population if a fire was uncontained. For example, Malheur County in Oregon has an AWHP of 2.85 but so few people live there that it's PW-AWHP is only 0.649.

This visualization utilizes Wildfire Hazard Potential(WHP) data produced by the USDA Forest Service Fire Modeling Institute with the goal "to depict the relative potential for wildfire that would be difficult for suppression resources to contain."
