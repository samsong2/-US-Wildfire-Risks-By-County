Wildfire Hazard Potential Visualization

Average WHP Score Population-Weighted Average WHP Score

This visualization utilizes Wildfire Hazard Potential (WHP) score data produced by the USDA Forest Service Fire Modeling Institute whose goal is "to depict the relative potential for wildfire that would be difficult for suppression resources to contain." The scale ranges from 0 to 5 with 5 being the highest risk. Scores are only produced for contiguous United States.
The average WHP is the average of all the scores registered for a specific county while the population-weighted average WHP is adjusted based on population density in a county, i.e. how much impact upon population if a fire was uncontained. For example, Malheur County in Oregon has an average WHP of 2.85 but so few people live there that it's adjusted WHP is only 0.649.
Hover over a specific county to see some additional demographic data points about it and toggle between the two maps to see the colors change for each county.
